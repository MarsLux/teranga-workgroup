A Third-Place for a Sustainable Future

TERANGA is a third-place (tiers-lieu) dedicated to the agricultural and food transition in the heart of Schifflange. Its mission is to foster encounters and exchanges on this central theme to collectively build a sustainable future.

The Project

Facing the failures of the global food system, which leaves 828 million people in hunger, plunges millions of farmers into extreme poverty, and depletes our planet, the transition to a sustainable agricultural and food system appears to be a necessity.

How can we feed the 10 billion people who will live on Earth by 2050 without destroying life? What dietary habits should we adopt? How can we, as consumers, reclaim power over our food in the face of purchasing power constraints? How can we support those who feed us? What role should politics play?

TERANGA aims to propose concrete answers to these questions and deepen the reflection with the residents of Schifflange and the surrounding areas through a committed program, in a pleasant and friendly setting.

A Place for Systemic Change

Our food system, increasingly based on agribusiness, is responsible for about 30% of greenhouse gas emissions, and intensive production methods are the leading cause of deforestation and biodiversity loss.

The path to a more sustainable world requires the transition to **agro-ecological practices** and a systemic, political, and societal change. **Food is a human right**, and it is up to society to influence the political framework to ensure fairer laws for all.

Community & Housing

TERANGA also hosts **eight rooms for young tenants (ages 18 to 26)** with low incomes, who pay affordable rent. In exchange for this housing, residents commit to dedicating approximately **five hours per week** to the project's activities. They share a common kitchen (also used for cooking workshops) and a living room (used for projections and meetings).

What can you do at TERANGA?

Inform and Debate

A thematic library and game library for both children and adults, and meetings to better understand the challenges of the food system.

Local Products

Enjoy on-site tasting or stock up through a deposit-sale system featuring local producers, members of the responsible food map.

Participate

Want to help build this project for your community? Donate a few hours of your time? **Contact us** to find out more about volunteering!

Events

A diverse and varied program awaits you: animations, workshops, evening gatherings, film-debates, concerts, exhibitions, and more.

Propose Ideas

Have an idea, a project, or a dream you'd like to see happen in this space? Participate in our citizen survey and let us know!

Relax

In a friendly space open to everyone, with no obligation to consume or participate in activities. Come and take a moment to unwind.
